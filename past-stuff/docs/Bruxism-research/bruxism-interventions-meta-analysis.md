## Executive Summary

This report synthesizes bruxism intervention research from three AI-generated sources (OpenAI, Claude, and Gemini) and cross-references their claims against current systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and clinical guidelines. The key finding: **no single intervention cures bruxism**, but a strategic combination of evidence-based approaches can effectively manage symptoms and prevent complications.

**Critical insight**: The original research sources varied in depth and accuracy. Claude's research was the most rigorous with proper evidence grading and citations to specific systematic reviews. OpenAI's research was comprehensive but less precise on evidence levels. Gemini's research included some useful mechanistic details but occasionally overstated evidence (e.g., claims about hypnotherapy and magnesium).

## Part 4: Recommended Action Plan

### Step 1: Immediate Actions (Week 1) — Cost: $0-20

1. **Sleep hygiene audit**: Consistent bedtime, no screens 1hr before bed, cool dark room
2. **Substance reduction**: No caffeine after noon, no alcohol within 4hrs of bed
3. **Jaw posture training**: "Lips together, teeth apart, tongue on palate" — set hourly phone reminders
4. **Self-massage**: Learn masseter and temporalis massage techniques (5 min, 2x daily)
5. **Warm compresses**: 15-20 min before bed

### Step 2: Professional Assessment (Weeks 2-4) — Cost: $150-400

1. **Dental evaluation**: Assess wear, fit for custom splint if needed
2. **Medication review**: If on SSRIs/SNRIs, discuss buspirone or alternatives
3. **GERD screen**: If symptoms present, trial PPI

### Step 3: Secondary Interventions (If Step 1-2 Insufficient) — Cost: Variable

1. **Custom hard acrylic splint**: Protects teeth, may reduce symptoms
2. **Professional massage**: Monthly sessions + continued self-massage
3. **Consider biofeedback**: If motivated and can commit to consistent use

### Step 4: Specialist Referral (If Refractory) — Cost: $500+

1. **Sleep study**: If OSA suspected (snoring, apneas, daytime fatigue)
2. **Botulinum toxin**: For severe pain/hypertrophy after conservative failure
3. **TMJ specialist**: If joint symptoms develop

## Key Takeaways

1. **Start free**: The highest-ROI interventions cost nothing
2. **Address root causes first**: Sleep disorders, GERD, stress, medications
3. **Splints protect, don't cure**: Valuable but not sufficient alone
4. **Botox works but costs**: Reserve for refractory cases; monitor bone health
5. **Supplements lack evidence**: Don't rely on magnesium/vitamin D for bruxism specifically
6. **Awake vs sleep bruxism differ**: Behavioral interventions work better for awake bruxism
7. **Combine approaches**: No single intervention is sufficient; multimodal approach is standard of care
